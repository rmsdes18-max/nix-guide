# Nix Expansion — Datamined Content

## Disclaimer

This document is based on datamined information. Item effects, values, names, classifications, and drop sources may change before release and may not appear in the live game exactly as listed.

This is datamined info, meaning it is what's in the code. Whether any or all of it actually comes through, it is subject to change. A lot of these numbers can change, and the scaling is definitely going to change. Some weapons might be stronger or weaker, they might rework the whole thing, we might not get any of these, or maybe they'll be slowly added. Treat everything here as just a general idea of what's coming with Nix.

This document was created in collaboration with **K9 Beat** and **Gros**. Hexapod got the video/leaks from Gros, so thank you Gros.

## General Info / How It Works

**What's coming with Nix:**

- **New Archboss weapons** — they seem very powerful.
- **New field boss items.**
- **New field items** — these kind of look like they'll be open-world items, like the open-world dungeon burning, maybe crack items. This might be a new open-world event, kind of similar to the Abyss event that came with the Abyss weapons.
- **Gear items** — Hexapod considers these like mini-passives on weapons. There are two different types of gear items in the file: normal gear items and enchantments.
  - **Gear items (mini-sockets):** The way it was described perfectly, you might be able to socket items into your weapon. Maybe a weapon can have two or three sockets, because some of these gear items can benefit each other — if you have gear item one, you do this, and then another gear item boosts the effect of gear item one. This is why he still considers it like two or three sockets on our weapons.
  - **Enchantments:** Just kind of small little quality-of-life improvements — like more damage or slightly more cooldown.
- **Skills and passives:** New skills and passives will be added in a new patch. Probably not additional skills but rather replacing existing skills, so we might be able to replace some of the skills we have.

## Longbow

### Gear Items

#### Eagle Eye
- **Effect:** Adds 1 stack each of Bullseye and Gale to targets hit by Strafing.
- **Hexapod:** With Strafing, by default you shoot two barrages; with the specialization you can shoot three barrages. Each barrage has four arrows, and each of those four arrows, when you proc Tempest, gets Tempest arrows. Normally only the original four-arrow barrage gets the Bullseye and Gale. So if you have the three-barrage specialization and hit them with all three, you put 12 stacks of Bullseye (four stacks per barrage). His question: does this add one to the four (so it becomes five instead of four), or is it like Tempest where each little Gale/Strafing barrage adds — so instead of up to 12 with three barrages, you could get 24? "Bro, that'd be insanity." We'll have to wait and see.

#### Infinite Strafing
- **Effect:** Changes Strafing to a toggle skill called Infinite Strafing, which fires Arrows without interruption. Decreases Base Mana cost by 300, but increases Mana cost by 100 with each Arrow fired. Does not apply to Specialization 2 [Directional Tempest] or Specialization 3 [Consecutive Use].
- **Hexapod:** His question is the fire rate — if it shoots slow (one arrow, one arrow), that might be pretty interesting; but if it's super fast, it could be interesting yet you'll burn through your mana. Looks interesting to be honest.

#### Pierce Shot
- **Effect:** Applies Weaken: Pierce Shot for 6s, which deals 25% of Base Damage + 25 damage per sec and reduces Healing Received by 80%.
- **Hexapod:** "Whoa, dude. We're getting anti-heal with bow, bro. That's insane." You already have long-range anti-shield with Deadly Marker, and now anti-heal too. "This is going to be insane." He's unsure what "Pure Shot" / "Pierce Shot" exactly refers to — "we'll see."

#### Torrent
- **Effect:** While on a Whirlpool, decreases Strafing and Zephyr's Nock skills' damage but transforms them into attacks that target up to 6 enemies. Strafing: each attack deals damage equal to 90% of Base Damage + 90 damage; Zephyr's Nock deals damage equal to 320% of Base Damage + 320 damage. Decreases PvP damage by 90% and increases damage against monsters by 50%.
- **Hexapod:** "So it becomes like an AoE cleave." Probably going to be for mob grinding. "You know what? I don't mind this. Maybe Longbow can finally have some use in mob grinding, which is fine."

#### Triple Snipe
- **Effect:** Changes Decisive Sniping to a movement skill called Triple Snipe that can be used 3 times. Damage now deals 166% of Base Damage + 166 damage. Does not apply to Specialization 1 [Increased Charging Time], Specialization 3 [Decreased Charging Time], or Specialization 4 [Directional Tempest].
- **Hexapod:** "It's a movement skill" — you backfire, shoot out of a Snipe and can use it two more times, kind of like Strafing. We'll have to wait and see for that one.

#### Wind God's Snipe
- **Effect:** Creates an aftershock 1s after the Decisive Sniping arrow passes, dealing 166% of Base Damage + 166 damage 3 times. Decreases PvP damage by 90% and increases damage against monsters by 50%.
- **Hexapod:** "That's fine." This probably puts a pole on the ground. If it penetrates through multiple targets, you put multiple stacks on the ground and do a lot of AoE damage. "I don't mind it."
